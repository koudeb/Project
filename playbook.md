# LinkedIn Organic Content Strategy Playbook (B2B SaaS)

## Step 1: Define a clear niche

Before creating content, define a specific target audience instead of a broad category.

For example:
- Bad: Marketing
- Good: B2B SaaS founders or growth marketers

Focus on one clear niche and avoid posting across multiple unrelated topics.

**Why this matters:** LinkedIn needs to quickly understand who your content is for. When your content is consistent and focused on one audience, the algorithm can categorize it better and show it to the right people. Posting across unrelated topics can reduce your reach because LinkedIn cannot clearly identify your audience.

(Source: Richard van der Blom, “Pick a lane” (clear niche), https://www.linkedin.com/posts/richardvanderblom_linkedin-doesnt-care-how-hard-you-worked-activity-7444649055810371584-GCWE/ , April 2026)

## Where experts disagree

### Disagreement 1: Content depth vs Immediate clarity

**Author A:** John Espirian says that long-form articles help improve discoverability and get cited more on LinkedIn. This shows that detailed and in-depth content can increase visibility.

(**Source:** John Espirian, https://www.linkedin.com/posts/johnespirian_a-lot-of-people-are-talking-about-this-new-activity-7438127966938644481--xG8/ , March 2026)

**Author B:** Richard van der Blom says that content should be clear immediately so LinkedIn can quickly understand what the post is about and show it to the right audience.

(**Source:** Richard van der Blom “Make it labelable in seconds”, https://www.linkedin.com/posts/richardvanderblom_linkedin-doesnt-care-how-hard-you-worked-activity-7444649055810371584-GCWE/ , April 2026)

**My take:** I believe clarity is more important for reach because LinkedIn needs to understand the content quickly. However, in-depth content is useful for building authority over time. The best approach is to keep content clear while also adding depth when needed.


---

### Disagreement 2: Value-first content vs Conversion-focused content

**Author A:** Amanda Natividad says content should be useful on its own and should not require a click to provide value. She emphasizes creating content that earns attention by being valuable and complete within the post.

(**Source:** Amanda Natividad, https://www.linkedin.com/posts/amandanat_a-friend-asked-me-whats-really-driving-my-activity-7443322215036547072-wXKl/ , April 2026)

**Author B:** Jasmin Alić shows that LinkedIn content, engagement, and profile positioning can be used together as a system to convert prospects into community members and clients.

(**Source:** Jasmin Alić, https://www.linkedin.com/posts/alicjasmin_i-stopped-caring-about-low-reach-on-linkedin-activity-7442170700154540032-Tnea/ , April 2026)

**My take:** I believe value-first content should be the foundation because it builds trust and engagement. However, combining it with a conversion system is important for business results. The best approach is to provide value first and then guide the audience toward conversion.


---

### Disagreement 3: Broad reach vs Targeted audience communication

**Author A:** Filipa Canelas emphasizes that LinkedIn content should build authority and reach a wider audience, as it influences AI-driven discoverability and helps buyers find you during their research process.

(**Source:** Filipa Canelas, https://www.linkedin.com/posts/filipa-canelas_linkedins-vp-of-marketing-shared-something-activity-7442225847295492096-N-TA/ , April 2026)

**Author B:** Ashley Faus says content should use audience-specific language and can be intentionally exclusive to connect with the right audience and build trust.

(**Source:** Ashley Faus, https://www.linkedin.com/posts/ashleyfaus_audience-language-activity-7436778110198398976-3_8y/ , March 2026)

**My take:** I believe targeted communication is more effective because it builds stronger trust and relevance with the right audience. However, building authority and reach is also important, so the best approach is to focus on a specific audience while consistently sharing valuable insights that establish expertise.
